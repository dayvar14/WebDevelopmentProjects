The legend of Verlantis goes that, a group of Alexandrians decided to set up their new civilization in the Sahara grasslands. However, unbeknownst to them, the land was treacherous, overrun with dangerous wildlife. After the people experienced what they call “The Great Dying”, they prayed to Marakim for a solution to their suffering. The Alexandrians cried day and night begging for refuge from the treacherous wasteland and all of its predators.

Marakim took pity on the Alexandrians and plucked an owlet from its mother. He cupped the bird in his hands and then threw it to the skies. The owlet spread its wings and grew instantaneously. It kept growing until the entire Alexandrian group was able to hold on its back. Marakim gave this animal a satchel and named it Verlantis. Eventually, the citizens developed a way to build a city and lived there ever since.
