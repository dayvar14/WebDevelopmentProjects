Long before the Egyptian empires, a group of ancient people named the Alexandrians lived in small paradise away far from the harsh conditions of the Sahara desert. Their lives were relatively simple, spending most of their time searching for water; desperately trying to survive in the dangerous and barren desert. The desert people worshiped their only god known only as Makirm, the God of the Desert. Makirm was born from the ruins of a fallen empire and was a savior to the vagrant people of the Sahara. Makirm witnessed the hardships of the people that worshipped him and took pity on them. He created 3 hermetical beasts and placed them throughout the desert.
The people, unaware of the Beasts of Makarim, continued living their laborious lives, shuffling from place to place in search of food and shelter. Occasionally, a gust of wind or a peculiar stone would guide the people one way or another until eventually, the people came upon the Beasts. Overcome with joy and astonishment, the people decided to give up their nomadic lifestyles and settle on the backs of each animal. The camel, whom the people named Avelias, supplies her people with water from her reserves in times of drought. Soleris, the turtle, lives in a watery oasis and provides her people with constant access to water and fertile fishing grounds. Verlantis, the owl, largest of the beasts, is home to those who are scavengers by nature. Verlantis is the protector of the other two beasts and is able to carry them to safety in times of drought. Together the people live peaceful, hypnagogic lives on the backs of their respective animals.
