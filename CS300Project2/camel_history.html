A group of Alexandrians, left homeless by the destruction of their once great city, developed a nomadic lifestyle and wandered into the Sahara desert in hopes of finding a great paradise to develop their new nation. The lengthy expedition eventually turned perilous, forcing the people into an never-ending struggle to find a sustainable water source. The people of Alexandria grew weary and began to drop dead one by one. Tired of the struggle, the group begged Marakim to save them from the perils of the desert. Saddened by the suffering of this nomadic group, Marakim decided to end this dehydration once and for all. He scooped up a wild camel from it's flock and wiped his tears onto the camel. The camel started to grow until it was large enough to fit an entire city upon its back.
